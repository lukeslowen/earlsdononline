Noble

Surname Origin: A nickname for someone of exceptionally graceful character, derived from "noble, notable" in Old French. Of French or Scots origin.

Our Noble family can trace its recent roots to the East Midlands of England and to Chesterfield, Derbyshire, in the 17th century. John Noble married Hannah Lee in Chesterfield in February 1655. Their son James Noble was born in 1656 and with wife Marie Ridge fathered Richard Noble, baptised in Chesterfield on July 24, 1693.

Richard married Esther Holland in Sheffield in 1716. After her death in 1734 he married Mary Swift. Their son Paul moved from Chesterfield to Derby where he practised as a staymaker — a specialised form of tailoring requiring knowledge of anatomy.

Paul met and married Elizabeth Jolley at St Werburgh, Derby on 10 June 1768. Their family continued growing through the East Midlands across subsequent generations, eventually settling in Nottingham where Thomas Noble and his descendants lived through the 19th and early 20th centuries.
