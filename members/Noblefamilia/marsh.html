Marsh

Surname Origin: An Anglo-Norman French word "marche" meaning boundary — a name for someone who lived on a border. Alternatively from the Teutonic "Maresche", meaning a tract of low, wet land.

Our Marsh family originate from Yorkshire and have been traced back to Sheffield at the end of the 18th century. Daniel Marsh was born in Sheffield in 1795, son of George and Rachel Marsh, christened in Sheffield parish church (later Sheffield Cathedral).

Daniel moved from Sheffield to Liverpool (via the United States) where he practised as a Butcher. In 1847 he appears in Manchester, then by 1848 the family had settled in Kingston upon Hull, where his son Charles Marsh was born.

By 1858 the Marsh family had moved to Nottingham. Charles, after serving in the army, returned to Nottingham and found employment as a lace maker. He married Henrietta Marshall in 1880. Their daughter Edith Marsh married George Noble.
