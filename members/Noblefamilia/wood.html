Wood

Surname Origin: Originally used to describe a person who lived in or worked in a wood or forest, from Middle English "wode". An ancient Scottish surname, first called De Bosco, because the family bore trees in their coat of arms.

Our earliest Wood ancestors have been traced to Lincolnshire. The family farmed at Housham Grange, a farm that features in the Drury family history, as William Blow — who took over the tenancy of Housham Grange — was connected to both families.

Richard Wood, born in 1761, is the earliest member we can identify directly. The Wood family were agricultural labourers and farmers in the Lincolnshire countryside over several generations.

The Wood family connect to the Drury family through Housham Grange, the Lincolnshire farm that provided a home and livelihood for both families during the 19th century. See also: Housham Grange.
