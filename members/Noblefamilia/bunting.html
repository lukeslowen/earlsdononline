Bunting

Surname Origin: Pre-Norman origin. The name of a kind of bird.

We have traced the Buntings back to William Bunting born in 1802. He and wife Mary settled in Mansfield, Nottinghamshire, having thirteen children between 1826 and 1850. It is possible both originated from Lincolnshire.

William, described as a plasterer, died one year after the birth of his last child. Their eldest son Walter Bunting also became a plasterer, marrying Louisa from Crewkerne, Somerset. The Bunting family connect to the Noble tree through Mary Ann Bunting, who married into the Noble line.
