Housham Grange

Housham, near South Hykeham, Lincolnshire

Housham Grange was a farm in the hamlet of Housham, close to South Hykeham, Lincolnshire — home to both the Blow and Drury families during the 19th century.

William Blow took over the tenancy around 1808 and farmed 100 acres by 1851. In his will of June 1854 he made specific provision for "his friend and old servant John Drury" to succeed him, marking a pivotal moment in the Drury family's fortunes.
