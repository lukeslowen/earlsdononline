Bill Drury

1867 – 1942

William (Bill) Drury served with the Leicestershire Regiment and saw extensive service across Ireland, France, Flanders, and at the Battle of Cambrai. His wartime diary and letters form a remarkable record of an ordinary soldier's experience of the Great War.

After the war Bill returned to Nottingham where he formed a relationship with May Fowler. They married at Staines Register Office in 1930 and their daughter Mavis Drury was born in 1928.
