Bill Drury — Lievin and Cambrai

1917

Lievin and Cambrai 1917

(text in italics is extracted from the 2/5 Leicesters War Diary)

The 177th Brigade eventually relieved the 3rd Canadian Brigade in the Avion sector. Conditions here were comparatively peaceful although in places the lines were very close.

13/10/1917 - 5pm march to Tottenham Camp take over front line from 48th Royal Canadian Highlanders.

14/10/1917 - Old Brewery . Front-line. Quiet, 1 killed and 2 wounded.*

15/10/1917 - Quiet on front line, occasional shelling on right.

16/10/1917 - 5am shelling.

17/10/1917 - Battalion relieved by 2/5 Lincolns and moved into support.

18/10/1917 - Piano Dug Out .

21/10/1917 - Relieved by 2/8 Sherwood Foresters and move at Alberta Camp .

22/10/1917 - Cleaning and equipping

23-27/10/1917 - Training and working parties.

28/10/1917 - British aeroplane crashed near D Coy HQ.

29/10/1917 - Lievin. Frontline, shelling and gassing. Relieved 2/6 North Staffs in line, joined by American army officer.

In Lievin one portion of some ruined house would be occupied by British troops whilst on the other side of the wall were the Germans. Communication between these forward posts and the front line was only possible by means of wires which were tugged according to a prearranged code. The only disturbing element during this period was an epidemic of gas attacks.

30/10/1917 - Quiet on the front.

31/10/1917 - Quiet. Gas projected in drums from our front at 6pm - no retaliation. Artillery shelled approaches east of Lens intermittently during the night to catch enemy relief - weather fine - aeroplanes active.

1/11/1917 - 41 officers and 724 other ranks.

2/11/1917 - 9am shelled for half an hour. 6pm Gas projected but no retaliation. 11pm Coy relief.

* Lance Corporal William Drury (241240). Wounded and reported in the War Office Weekly Casualty List on 3 November 1917.

3/11/1917 - Some gas dropped between reserve and supported Coys. Aerial activity nil as visibility bad. Three other ranks gassed.

4/11/1917 - Quiet morning. visibility fair. Support Coy shelled with gas. Our barrage shelled from 6pm to 6am. 2 other ranks killed 1 gassed.

5/11/1917 - Visibility fair - retaliation by our heavies for enemy trench mortar activity at night. Midnight, patrols in search of enemy machine gun.

6/11/1917 - Visibility fair - battalion relieved by 2/4 Lincolns.

7/11/1917 - Battalion in support. Working parties.

8/11/1917 - 11.50am, visibility good, aerial activity. A few high explosives fell on main road near Coys billets.

9/11/1917 - Visibility good, aerial activity. HE shrapnel over our area at 3 minute intervals from 9am - 3pm. 6pm gas projected - no retaliation.

10/11/1917 - Normal.

11/11/1917 - Bosche aeroplane shot down.

12/11/1917 - Normal.

On November 13th the Canadians returned to the line and the 59th Division was removed for special training around Chateau de la Haie, a large house concealed in the woods behind the line. This training was chiefly devoted to 'open warfare' exercises (as opposed to 'trench to trench attacks') in preparation for the 'Tank Attack' on Cambrai. It was hoped that this attack would create a breakthrough and the 59th Division was to be held in readiness to help exploit it.

13/11/1917 - Relieved by 4th Canadian Battalion. Battalion moved by decauville light railway and march to Petit Servins arrive at 3am..

14/11/1917 - Cleaning and equipping.

15-16/11/1917 - Training.

17/11/1917 - 12.30pm move to Habarcq arrive at 5pm

18/11/1917 - Church parade

19/11/1917 - 4.30pm march to Bailleulval via Gouy-en Artois arriving at 8.30pm.

20/11/1917 - Battalion bathed.

21/11/1917 - Training and equipping. 11pm march via Berles au Bois , Bienvillers Au Pois , Bucquey to Achiet-Le-Petit , Henham Camp South arrive 6am.

Whilst the attack took place the Brigade was held in reserve and marches were made at night in order to avoid the observation of German spotter planes. After spending two or three days in preparation the whole Division was suddenly transferred to the 4th Corps and ordered to relieve the Guards at Bourlon Woods.

22/11/1917 - Rest

23/11/1917 - Entrained at Aichiet-Le Grand at 10am for Fins 1pm march to Dessart Wood.

24-26/11/1917 - Training.

27/11/1917 - Route march to Trescaulclt from 2.30pm - 4pm.

By November 28th the line was an unpleasant salient with enemy shells falling from all sides. Available cover was in short supply and there was a great deal of aerial activity.

28/11/1917 - 2.30pm march to Flesquires via Ribecourt .

29/11/1917 - Flesquires. Quartered in dug outs. Aerial activity.

30/11/1917 - 11am activity all along front - 4 Bosche aeroplanes shot down. One enemy and one British observation balloon shot down. Two other ranks wounded. 5.30pm moved forward to occupy Hindenburg Support Line just outside village - occasional shelling.

An enemy attack was launched against the Stafford Brigade in Bourlon Wood. This had been preceded by an intense concentration of gas shells. An attack on the 177th Brigade was also expected to the right of Bourlon Wood but it never came. In fact the enemy had successfully attacked through Gouzeaucourt and was threatening the rear of the whole 59th Division. The forward troops were happily ignorant of their predicament. It became clear that the forward positions were untenable and there was a general withdrawal to the Flesquieres area.

1/12/1917 - 11am Activity along the whole front. Visibility good.

2/12/1917 - Battalion relieved 2/5 South Staffs in right front line, HQ Cantaing Mill .

3/12/1917 - Cantaing Mill visibility good - aerial activity.

4/12/1917 - 4am battalion withdrew to hold Flesquieres Line . HQ in German dug out under church. 2 other ranks wounded.

5/12/1917 - Quiet.

It was not until 3pm on December 6th that the outpost line in front of Flesquieres was definitely attacked. The outpost line withdrew fighting. The enemy attack eventually collapsed and prisoners were taken.

6/12/1917 - Flesquieres Church. 3pm enemy attack in large numbers. 59 Division outpost line withdrawn. Enemy repulsed with heavy losses - one officer killed 4 other ranks 26 wounded.

Apart from artillery fire the next few days were without incident and the Brigade was withdrawn on December 10th.

7/12/1917 - Heavy shelling

8/12/1917 - Artillery activity increased.

The weather turned very cold with severe frost and snow covering the country. It made work on new trenches a slow process and encouraged German spotter planes on moonlit night raids.

9/11/1917 - Trescault . Heavy artillery duels at intervals. Prisoners brought in. 12 noon, Battalion relieved by 2/8 Sherwoods.

10/11/1917 - In support in the old line. 12 noon, relieved by 2/4 Leicesters, proceeded to Lechelle , arrive at 4pm.

11/12/1917 - Lechelle. Equipping and cleaning. Casualties, 2 officers and 58 other ranks (5 killed).

Lechelle offered a brief rest despite the constant enemy air-raids and the intense cold.

12-13/12/1917 - Training.

14/12/1917 - 11.55am move to support at Trescauclt , relieving 2/4 Leicesters.

15/12/1917 - Trescauclt . Working parties, heavy shelling by 5.9 battery.

16/12/1917 - Transport lines moved from Neuville to Bertincourt . Battalion supplies working parties.

17/12/1917 - Relieve 2/8 Sherwoods in Hindenburg Support Line (right).

18-19/12/1917 - Hindenburg Support Line. Working parties, desultory artillery fire on both sides

20-21/12/1917 - Working parties, desultory artillery fire. One other rank killed, two wounded.

22/12/1917 - 6pm relieved by 12th Manchester Rgt proceed to Butlers Cross .

Final relief from the front came on December 23rd when the whole Division was relieved and withdrawn to for rest and refitting.

23/12/1917 - At 10.30am relieved by 10th West Ridings. Proceeded to Rocquigny arriving at 2pm.

24/12/1917 - Equipping and cleaning.

25/12/1917 - 5.30am march to Bapaume entrained at 10am to Tinques . March to rest billets at 3pm.

26/12/1917 - Resting and cleaning at Penin . Heavy snow.

Penin was literally miles back from the line. It was Christmas and there was snow on the ground and parcels and letters to open. Whilst resting here rooms in estaminets and private houses were hired as reading and recreation rooms and troops were made comfortable.

27/12/1917 - 10am kit inspection. Celebrated Christmas.

28-29/12/1917 - Training.

30/12/1917 - Parade.

31/12/1917 - Inspection of billets.
