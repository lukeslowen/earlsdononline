Ken Noble

1925 – 2002

Ken Noble was born at 5 Guy's Terrace, Blue Bell Hill Road, Nottingham on 22 November 1925, the first child of George and Edith Noble. His childhood was badly disrupted by the death of his mother when he was just seven years old.

As a teenager Ken joined the Nottingham Boys Brigade, learning to play drums — a skill that proved useful throughout his life. At the outbreak of war in 1939, aged 14, he left school and took up factory work.

Ken joined the Royal Navy and served during the Second World War, seeing action in the East Indies and Far East. He served aboard HMS Rochester, HMS Boxer, and HMS Formidable, and witnessed the Japanese surrender in 1945.

After the war Ken worked in Nottingham's pub trade, eventually becoming landlord of the Wheatsheaf. He died in 2002 at the age of 76.
