Ward

Surname Origin: An occupational name for a guard or watchman, from Old English weard. Also a geographical name for a person who lived near a guardhouse or fortress.

Ward has been a common name in Leicestershire during the last 200 years. Our earliest family member is John Ward who married Sarah James in Shepshed, Leicestershire in October 1770. They had twelve children.

The family were framework knitters — part of the great hosiery industry of Leicestershire that dominated the area throughout the 18th and 19th centuries. The stocking frame was central to family life and livelihoods across several generations.

The Ward family connect to the Noble family tree through Louisa Lloyd, who was the daughter of John Lloyd and Louisa Ward.
