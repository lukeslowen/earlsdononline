Floyd

Surname Origin: English variant of the Welsh "Lloyd" meaning grey and hairy. The English found it difficult to pronounce the Welsh Ll sound and interpreted it as Fl.

Our Floyds appear to originate from Staffordshire where Abraham Floyd married Ann Taylor at Norton in the Moors, Staffordshire in July 1797. Their son Nathan was born in Staffordshire and the family later moved to Nottinghamshire.

The Floyd family connect to the Noble tree through Nottingham, where several generations settled and intermarried with other local families.
