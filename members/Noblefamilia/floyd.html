Floyd

Surname Origin: English variant of the Welsh "Lloyd" meaning grey and hairy. The English found it difficult to pronounce the Welsh Ll and interpreted it as Fl.

Our Floyds originate from Staffordshire where Abraham Floyd married Ann Taylor at Norton in the Moors in July 1797.

The Floyd family connect to the Noble tree through Nottingham, where several generations settled and intermarried.
