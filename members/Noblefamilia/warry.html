Warry

Surname Origin: The origin remains a mystery, but one theory suggests it could be of Cornish/Welsh origin meaning "of an important place".

Our Warrys are deeply rooted in Somerset around the town of Chard. There is reference to a William atte Warrye of Chard in 1327 as a contributor to the Lay Subsidy of Edward I. The first individual from whom a direct line can be identified is Stephen Warry of Chard who died in 1530.

The Warry family were weavers and merchants in Chard across many generations. Rebecca Warry married James Fowler (senior) in 1863, bringing the Warry and Fowler families together.
