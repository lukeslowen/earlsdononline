Wainwright

Surname Origin: A waggon maker — from Old English "waegn" (waggon) and "wyrhta" (maker or worker).

So far we have traced our Wainwright line back to William Wainwright who married Jane Barber at St Martins in Worcester in December 1813. William was a bricklayer.

The couple moved from Worcester to Gloucester and had several children. The family eventually moved to Nottingham where they connect to the broader Noble family story.
