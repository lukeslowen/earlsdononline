Drury

Surname Origin: The Drury name in England dates back to the time of William the Conqueror in 1066 AD. The first mention comes from the Norman Conquest when John de Drury (de Roueray), a knight in William's army, accompanied him from France to England.

Our Drury family is well rooted in Lincolnshire and can be traced back to Robert Drewry, born about 1685. He married Catherine Hanson of Bottesford, Leicestershire. Their son Robert, born in Brant Broughton, Lincolnshire, became Sheriff of Lincoln and its Mayor in 1761.

William Drury, born in 1744 in Brant Broughton, was a shoemaker. His son John became a farm servant for William Blow, farmer at Housham Grange, Lincolnshire — a connection that would prove pivotal when Blow's will made specific provision for "his friend and old servant John Drury" to succeed him.

John's son William Drury (1833–1891) moved the family from rural Lincolnshire to Nottingham. His sons Tom Drury and William (Bill) Drury both served in the First World War.
