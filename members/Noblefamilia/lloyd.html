Lloyd

Surname Origin: From the Welsh "llwyd" meaning grey or hoary.

Our Lloyds originate in Worcestershire with the birth in 1780 of Richard Loyd, youngest son of William Loyd and Elizabeth Wedgeborrow. Richard's son John was born in Claines, Worcestershire in 1804.

John Lloyd, a blacksmith, married Mary Ann Edwards of Claines in 1830. The family moved to Mansfield in Nottinghamshire around 1836, and later to Derby. Their daughter Louisa Lloyd married into the Ward family, connecting the Lloyds to the broader family tree.
