Whitaker

Surname Origin: Habitational name from places named with Old English hwit "white" or hwæte "wheat" + æcer "cultivated land" — meaning "white field".

Our Whitaker family line dates from John Whitaker, born in Mansfield Woodhouse in about 1818. John's mother was Ann Sissons and his father was Sampson Slaney, but John took his mother's married name Whitaker.

John Whitaker formed a relationship with Eliza Fowler (née Peck) in Nottingham and together they had three children, including May Fowler. As Eliza was still married to James Fowler, John and Eliza could not wed, but John was a devoted partner until his death in 1909.
