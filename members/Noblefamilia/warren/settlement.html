Settlement Certificate DE812/26/23

Thomas Warren, 1742

This is the original settlement certificate for Thomas Warren, Anne his wife and their three children, dated 7 January 1742, confirming their legal settlement in the Parish of Ratcliff Culey, Leicestershire.

County of Leicester — to wit.

We John Eaton and John Joyce, Church-wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Sheepy Magna in the County of Leicester aforesaid, do hereby own and acknowledge Thomas Warren, Anne his wife and their three children to be our Inhabitants legally settled in the Parish of Ratcliff Culey aforesaid.

In Witness whereof, we have hereunto set our Hands and Seals, this Seventh Day of January in the sixteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Second by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, and in the Year of our Lord 1742.

Attested by Richard Chaplin & William Darby
Signed: John Eaton & John Joyce

To the Church-wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Sheepy Magna in the County of Leicester or to any or either of them.

We whose Names are hereunto subscribed, two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace for the County of Leicester aforesaid, do allow of the above-written Certificate. And we do also Certify, that Richard Chaplin one of the Witnesses who attended the Execution of the said Certificate, hath made Oath before Us, that he did see the Church-wardens and Overseers, whose Names and Seals are to the said Certificate subscribed and set, severally sign and seal the said Certificate, and that the Names of the said Richard Chaplin and William Darby whose Names are above subscribed, as Witnesses to the Execution of the said Certificate, are of their own proper Hand-writing.

Dated the seventh Day of January in the Year of our Lord 1742.

This document confirms the Warren family's long-established connection with the parishes of Sheepy Magna and Ratcliff Culey in Leicestershire, providing valuable evidence for the family's roots in this area in the early eighteenth century.
