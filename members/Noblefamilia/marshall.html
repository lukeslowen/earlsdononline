Marshall

Surname Origin: Mare (horse) servant — covering occupations including farrier, groom, and horse doctor. A name of office: master of the horse.

Our Marshall roots trace to Joseph Marshall, born in Derby in 1796. The family were rooted in the Midlands with connections to Staffordshire and Nottinghamshire.

Henrietta Marshall married Charles Marsh in Nottingham in 1880. Through Henrietta and Charles, the Marshall name connects to the Noble family in the 20th century.
