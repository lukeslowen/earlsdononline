Marshall

Surname Origin: Mare (horse) servant — possibly meaning a wide variety of related occupations including farrier, groom, and horse doctor. A name of office: master of the horse.

Our Marshall roots have been traced back to Joseph Marshall, who according to the census was born in Derby in 1796. The family were rooted in the Midlands, with connections to Staffordshire and Nottinghamshire.

Henrietta Marshall, born around 1855, married Charles Marsh in Nottingham in 1880. Her family had been established in Nottingham as lace workers, part of the great textile industry that defined the city in the Victorian era.

Through Henrietta Marshall and Charles Marsh, the Marshall name connects to the Noble family in the 20th century.
